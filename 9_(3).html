Imleria badia, commonly known as the bay bolete
is an edible, pored mushroom found in Europe and North America, where it grows
in coniferous or mixed woods on the ground or on decaying tree stumps, sometimes

prolific numbers. Both the common and scientific names refer to the bay- or chestnut-coloured cap, which is almost spherical in young specimens before broadening and flattening out to a diameter up to 15 cm (6 in). On the cap underside are small yellowish pores that turn dull blue-grey when bruised. The smooth, cylindrical stipe, measuring 4–9.
cm (1.6–3.5 in) long by 1–2 cm (0.4–0.8 in) thick, is coloured like the cap, but paler. Some varieties have been described from eastern North America.
